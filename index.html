The Concept

A hidden world.
An unwanted bond.
An impossible choice.

Vessel is a contemporary urban thriller set in London, built on an original mythology of immortal entities that have existed alongside humanity since the dawn of civilisation. These beings feed on human emotion, drawing sustenance from every feeling a person experiences across a lifetime, and have done so since the dawn of time.

Most humans never know they exist. Some are chosen.

What makes Vessel distinct is its moral architecture. There are no good supernatural beings and no evil ones. The ancient being who chose the protagonist is not a guardian angel. She feeds from him, regulates his emotions without his knowledge, and cares about his survival mainly because his death would undo everything she has built. When she begins to feel something beyond self interest, it becomes the emotional engine of the story.

Genre: Supernatural Thriller  •  Dark Fantasy  •  Action

The Mythology

Humanity has never
been alone.

Since before the first civilisations, an ancient species has existed alongside humanity. Immortal, invisible to ordinary perception, and sustained entirely by human emotion. They are not gods in the conventional sense. They do not answer prayers or grant wishes.

I
Tier I — The Gods
Cosmic architects. They created the conditions for life and feed on the collective emotional output of entire civilisations. Humanity is to them what ants are to the planet. The religions and gods humanity has named across history are misunderstood residue of something real and far less personal.
II
Tier II — The Bonded
Intimate and deeply invested in individual human lives. Each carries an essence, a specific emotional frequency they can control within their chosen human. The bond is permanent, deepening through shared experience, capable of becoming the most significant relationship either of them has ever had.
∞
Primordials
The near-gods. Ancient beyond reckoning, capable of world-altering power, regarded by Tier I with something resembling familial respect. They do not need a Vessel but prefer one. A Primordial choosing a human is therefore an event of enormous significance. The human was not chosen out of necessity.
○
Strays — The Unbound
Any entity without a human bond. Unbound, hungry, and desperate. The hidden explanation behind centuries of folklore. Unexplained deaths. The feeling of being watched in empty places. The legends are real. The explanations are not.
◆
The Essence
Each entity carries an essence, a specific emotional frequency they can control within their chosen human. Not what they feed on, they feed on everything equally, but what they can regulate. A fear essence can suppress or amplify fear. A grief essence can accelerate or deepen loss.
▲
The Bond
Permanent once formed. The human has no say and may never know it is happening. It deepens through shared experience, capable of becoming the most significant relationship either of them has ever had. An entity that feels too much, that wants too much, that loses control of its own emotional restraint, can destroy the very thing it was trying to keep.

The Vision

A world built
to last.

Vessel is designed as the foundation of an expanding world rather than a single contained story. The mythology is deep enough to sustain years of storytelling. The characters are specific enough to carry an audience across multiple formats.

Film One — In Development
Vessel
Daniel, a directionless young Londoner, is chosen by Noel, one of the most powerful entities alive, as her human bond. When a secret organisation discovers what he carries, he is forced into a hidden world of immortal beings, forbidden power, and a relationship neither of them was prepared for. A complete story. A door left open.
Film Two — In Development
Vessel: Title Pending
In the aftermath of the first film, Daniel is powerless and grieving. Noel is somewhere in the world, a Stray, carrying the memory of everything they were without knowing why she cannot stop thinking about a human whose name she no longer knows. The mission is finding her. The emotional question: whether a bond that was never chosen can be chosen again, deliberately, with full knowledge of what it costs.
Companion Series — Planned
Vessel: Hunt
Episodic, grounded, horror adjacent. Each episode follows Daniel and Noel hunting unbound entities in the hidden spaces of contemporary London and beyond. The series format allows the world to breathe week to week, telling contained human stories within the larger mythology. The entry point for audiences who discover the property through streaming rather than cinema.

Visual Identity

Tone & Visual
Language

Grounded
Contemporary London, ordinary and unglamorous. The baseline the supernatural is measured against.
Layered
The supernatural exists beneath the ordinary. Something is wrong before the audience can name it.
Horror
Strays are corrupted and wrong. The horror comes from wrongness.

Vessel is grounded before it is supernatural. The visual language of the first act is deliberately ordinary. Fluorescent office lighting. Grey morning commutes. A flat that is functional rather than lived in. The mundanity is intentional. It is the baseline against which everything that follows lands harder.

As the bond deepens the visual world shifts without announcing itself. London does not become fantastical. It becomes layered. The same streets carry a different weight. The same faces hide different things. The supernatural does not replace the ordinary. It exists beneath it.

Noel's avatar is precise and timeless, almost elvish, a presence that does not belong to any particular era. When she manifests publicly the camera treats her the way it treats something genuinely ancient in a modern space. Slightly wrong in the best possible way. Too still. Too aware. Her true form is not shown directly. It is felt. Sound design and peripheral suggestion rather than spectacle.

The Stray sequences are where the film earns its horror credentials. A Stray's avatar is unstable and corrupted. Darkened veins. Grayed skin. Proportions that slip when it is hungry. Shot tightly and practically, close to the ground, the horror coming from wrongness rather than scale.

The action is grounded and consequential. Daniel is not a superhero in the conventional sense, though some of what he can do would be difficult to describe any other way. The capabilities the bond gives him come entirely from Noel, granted at her discretion and shaped by what she is.

Dark without being nihilistic. Supernatural without being fantastical. Emotionally driven without sacrificing momentum.

The Characters

Protagonist — The Vessel
Daniel
Twenty seven. British. Works a desk job that pays the rent and asks nothing of him. Raised with military discipline and a mother who valued character over comfort. Decent in a way that is not performed or self conscious. Cannot make himself look away when something wrong is happening in front of him.

The Primordial — Aspect of Fear
Noel
Existed since before the planet had its current form. Almost elvish in appearance, with very straight golden hair and a stillness that makes rooms feel different. Stoic. Speaks when she has something to say. Capable of warmth toward the very few things she has chosen to care about across incomprehensible time.
Potential fit: To be cast (twin?)
The Romantic Interest
Lucy
Mid twenties. Works in the same office as Daniel. Warm, feminine and naturally grounded, with a clear sense of identity and boundaries. Slow to trust, but deeply loyal once trust is given. She is the one who remains at the end.


Potential fit: To be cast
The Antagonist
Adrian
Mid forties. Former military and intelligence. Charismatic in the controlled way of men trained to operate under pressure. His diagnosis is often correct. His conclusions are not.

The Antagonist's Primordial
Corvin
An ancient Primordial. Appears human in his late twenties, with a contained, martial presence that signals danger instinctively. His relationship with Adrian is transactional rather than loyal. He has ambitions older than the organisation he serves.

The Sister — Most Powerful Entity
Nael
Identical to Noel in appearance, except for white hair. A being of such power that comparison becomes meaningless. Not aligned as villain or ally. The oldest presence in the world, and the only constant in Noel's existence.
